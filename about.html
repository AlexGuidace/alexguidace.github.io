Forever a Student

I see learning to code as a journey of self-improvement and utility. It's never-ending and the path is fraught with tough and challenging problems that need solving--that's part of what makes it fun. There's a certain satisfaction that comes from learning how to, and then making a computer do something interesting or useful. Oftentimes, the code written is interacting with itself on multiple levels in complex ways--and to see the end product work seamlessly after hours of learning, debugging, and testing to make sure it works right, is indeed deeply satisfying.

My Path

I started out with Java, basically without a clue as to what I was doing. With Java, I took courses, built projects, and worked with it on the job. I completed coursework on general computer science fundamentals using other languages like C and Python. Eventually, I decided that I needed to narrow my expertise as a software developer, so I branched off into web development before working with it on the job.

I chose web development because of its vast learning resources and job demand, and truly because I've always been interested in the magic that is the internet--the thing that has enabled me to research whatever I want through a few keystrokes, engage in discourse with friends and strangers on any topic, and do some of the things I most love, like play video games.

Today, I am investing my time in strengthening my web development foundations: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I know that any frameworks or technologies I will use in this field going forward stand on the shoulders of these giants.
