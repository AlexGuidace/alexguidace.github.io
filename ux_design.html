The User Experience

Back in 2017, I read the fantastic book The Design of Everyday Things by Don Norman.

I was fascinated by the fact that some doors had handles that were "designed to pull" while others were "designed to push." I had no idea that there was an entire field devoted to addressing such considerations: Usability!

This book inspired me to read other books on usability design and user experience.

I eventually decided that I wanted to pursue a career in making people’s use of technology a pleasurable, simple, and fun affair.

I created some UX projects and put them up in a portfolio.

The career didn’t pan out, mostly because the opportunity to become a software developer presented itself, but I still hold a soft spot for UX design in my heart, and delight when I find greatly-considered UX design in my daily life.

I even enjoy a bad user experience, since such events show me how important the field really is.

A great plus—this affinity for UX has carried over into the software I write; I really try to consider the end user when I am writing code for apps I am creating or updating.
